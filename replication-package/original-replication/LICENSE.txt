1. DHS (Demographic Health Survey):
The DHS dataset is shared under a custom restricted-use license governed by the DHS Program.
- Usage: Data are provided free of charge for legitimate academic, research, and policy purposes. Users must agree to the DHS Terms of Use and obtain registration approval.
- Restrictions: Commercial usage is prohibited, and users must not share raw data with unauthorised individuals.
 
2. IPUMS (Integrated Public Use Microdata Series):
IPUMS retains copyright over their packaged data extracts and shares them under IPUMS-specific terms.
- Usage: Data are licensed for scholarly research and educational purposes only. Explicit commercial use is strictly prohibited, and all users must register and agree to an annual terms-of-use agreement. Redistribution to non-registered individuals is not allowed.
- Restrictions: These are treated as internal-use-only, meaning extracts are available exclusively to approved users, and sharing is strictly prohibited. Confidentiality must be maintained, including non-disclosure of direct identifiers.
 
3. MICS (Multiple Indicator Cluster Surveys):
A UNICEF-specific memorandum of understanding governs MICS data use and data use terms.
- Usage: Data are provided free of charge for non-commercial, research, academic, and policy use. Access requires registration and approval from UNICEF or national authorities.
- Restrictions: Users must safeguard respondent confidentiality, not redistribute the datasets, and properly cite UNICEF MICS in all outputs.

4. SACMEQ (Southern Africa Consortium on Monitoring Educational Quality):
The SACMEQ data archive is governed by the Southern and Eastern Africa Consortium for Monitoring Educational Quality Co-ordinating Centre.
- Usage: Data are provided free of charge to eligible users; users must agree to the “Terms and Conditions for the Use of the SEACMEQ Data Archive” 
- Restrictions: Commercial usage is prohibited. Users must not share raw data with those not registered, and users shall give due credit to SEACMEQ for providing the Data Archive in all outputs.

5. World Bank Development Indicators:
The World Bank Group makes data publicly available according to open data standards and licenses datasets under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International license (CC-BY 4.0).
- Usage: Users can copy, modify, and distribute data in any format for any purpose, including commercial use. CC-BY 4.0 is the default license for all Datasets produced by the World Bank itself and distributed as open data.
- Restrictions: obligated to give appropriate credit (attribution) and indicate if any changes were made, including translations.
